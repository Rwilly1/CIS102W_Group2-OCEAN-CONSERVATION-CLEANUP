Get Involved

Regardless of your age or background, you can help bring us one step closer to making the ocean a better place.

How Can You Help?

Making the world a better place doesn't have to be hard. With just a few changes to your lifestyle, you can improve your environment.

Lower Plastic Use

Plastic is the largest source of pollution in our oceans, with over 358 trillion microplastic particles floating just on the ocean's surface. Making small changes to your life, such as using reusable shopping bags or bottles, and opting for plastic-free alternatives can significantly reduce your plastic use.

Reduce Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide and methane can trap heat from the sun, thus increasing the ocean's temperature and disturbing it's ecosystem. Just by lowering everyday habits like driving and electricity consumption, you can reduce your carbon footprint.

Reduce Chemical Use

Toxic chemicals are one of the leading causes of pollution, which can easily be reduced by avoiding the use of pesticides, fertilizers, and finding environmentally-friendly alternatives.

If You Want to Make a Bigger Impact..

Beach Cleanups

Volunteers are ALWAYS welcome. Regardless of age, you can participate in a beach cleanup and make our beaches and shores a better place for humans and marine animals alike. If you want to learn more, feel free to contact us today!

Advocate for Organizations

Spreading the word and supporting non-profit organizations working to reduce pollution in the ocean is a great way to to help combat the damage done to our oceans by inviting more people to join our cause.

Support Legislation to Combat Ocean Pollution

While working together as a community to improve our oceans is important, we also need legislation that improves waste management, adopts smart ocean policies and fights against climate change. For more information on legislation that can help better our environment, you can visit www.epa.gov.
